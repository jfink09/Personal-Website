ResearchFirst Startup

questuni.com

I had my first attempt at creating a startup with two of my roomates from college. Our goal was to help undergraduate students get involved in research projects that were of greatest interest to them in order to help increase innovation within Penn State, the community, and the world. One of the greatest mistakes we made was spending too much time on customer development instead of building, testing, and iterating a minimum viable product (MVP). During our customer discovery, we discovered three themes that research professors and undergraduate students kept telling us. 1) There was a lack of knowledge regarding the research opportunities that existed and how students can benefit and be involved in research, 2) students expectations of research did not match professors expectations of the student, and 3) professors had a difficult time, in general, vetting or finding the right students for their projects.

Our product was to have students fill out a basic form on our website with questions that were specific to the students current skills, knowledge, and interests. We took their information and developed a report containing several research projects that most closely matched with the student's responses. Although this project did not work out, I learned so much about the process of building a startup and learned what not to do the next time I build a startup. This startup recieved a grant from Penn State's accelerator program called the Summer Founder's Program.
