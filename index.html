Aerospace Engineer

I graduated with honors in aerospace engineering from The Pennsylvania State University in May 2021. Here, I performed two years of academic research; one year in biomedical engineering and one year in fluid mechanics. I had the opportunity to intern for an aircraft structures company in 2019 and completed the pre-medicine curriculum. I wrapped up my undergraduate career by completing a thesis for my fluid mechanics research concerning design optimizations for turbulence reduction and flow separation prevention of a closed-circuit, compressed air wind tunnel and presented it at the AIAA Regional Conference at Rutger's University.

Current Work

I am currently working as an Ophthalmology Medical Technician while I apply to medical schools with the intention of starting this fall. We do a lot of different scans on patients, and these scans led me to a project I am currently working on called DeepFundus. This is a deep learning platform for medical students, residents, and ophthalmologists to use for retinal disease prediction. Users can upload funduscopic images and the model will predict what disease it is. I plan to extend this to occular coherence tomography (OCT) scans once my model is trained well on the majority of the major retinal diseases that can be diagnosed by funduscopic image testing.

I am also actively trading and managing my portfolio everyday. This year I have many goals, but the most important ones revolve around excellent risk management, sticking to my routines and my buy and sell rules, tracking my trades in my tracker and log, marking up every trade, and market outperformance. I am also attempting to complete the TraderLion chart challenge which will require me to markup a chart everyday for the year. With this challenge, I am marking up weekly charts from the 1920s to the 2000s. My plan is to focus on charts from decades ago to learn more about past market history that I did not partake in. Also, I am making an effort to include charts of topping patterns and some of history's greatest shorting opportunities because we very rarely see chart markups for the short side. Because weekly charts do not capture the entry points as well as daily charts, I am also marking up the entry on each chart's daily chart. This is to learn what to expect for an ideal entry from the daily timeframe. My charts can be followed on my Twitter page Jason_Fink9. I enjoy posting charts on Twitter as it documents charts I am watching or thinking about at the time I post them.

I enjoy spending my free time reading books about trading, philosophy, and engineering, playing piano, exercise, and meditation. I am starting to learn more about algorithmic trading and will be working on multiple algorithms. This will imporve my programming abilities and perhaps allow me to capture alpha in another way than my current discretionary methods.
